Problems we are Addressing

Producing one kilogram of leather requires about 17,000 liters of water.

Processing one ton of raw hides produces 200-250 kg of solid waste, often ending up in landfills.

Hexavalent chromium, a byproduct of tanning, is carcinogenic and poses serious health risks.

About Us

The leather industry results in excessive carbon emissions, immense water usage, and the production of toxic waste as a byproduct of the tanning process. Feels is a material innovation company dedicated to creating sustainable, plant-based leather alternatives using Felt Fibre.

At Feels, our mission is to redefine sustainability in the textile industry by creating high-quality, eco-friendly felt fibers from agricultural waste. We are dedicated to transforming sugarcane, corn, mango waste, and other natural by-products into innovative and sustainable materials that benefit both people and the planet.

Our Approach

Felt Fiber from
Mango Waste

Mango waste, once discarded, now finds new life in the form of sustainable felt fiber. This biodegradable material not only prevents waste but also opens up opportunities for eco-friendly textile solutions.

Innovative Fiber from
Corn Waste

Crafted from discarded corn husks, this fiber is a testament to sustainable innovation. By repurposing agricultural waste, we create versatile, biodegradable felt that minimizes environmental impact and promotes a greener future.

Sustainable Felt from
Sugarcane Waste

Derived from sugarcane residue, this eco-friendly felt fiber reduces agricultural waste while contributing to a circular economy. The fibers are durable, biodegradable, and perfect for a range of sustainable textile applications.

Sustainable Felt from
pineapple Waste

Made from pineapple leaves, this eco-friendly felt fiber offers strength and durability while reducing agricultural waste. By repurposing these natural fibers, we create a sustainable material that supports ethical production and promotes a greener future.
